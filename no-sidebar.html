What We Do

Pillar amasses donations into an investment fund, creating a source of productive credit for small business in Central Illinois to further local economic development. Pillar then provides credit to local entrepreneurs and small businesses that would benefit from responsible credit use in order to grow. Prospects are evaluated through an online and in-person vetting process before being matched with an MBA student advisory team to create loan proposals, specific and credible plans for loan usage, and an application subject to final approval from Pillar’s allocation committee. After the loan is disbursed, Pillar provides ongoing advisory services to enhance the chances of success.

Why We Do it

Pillar’s mission is to support and amplify the economy and entrepreneurial ecosystem of Central Illinois by confederating local capital, business expertise, and community entrepreneurs while providing real-world business education experience for members of the Illinois business school.

This investment fund bridges the University of Illinois with the Champaign-Urbana community, and therefore benefiting a wide array of stakeholders including students, companies, community, and contributors. Most notably, local companies gain access to low-interest credit and free business consulting while the community will benefit through sustaining additional jobs and services.
